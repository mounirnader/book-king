1984 by GEORGE ORWELL

War and revolution have left the world unrecognisable. Great Britain, now known as Airstrip One, is ruled by the Party, led by Big Brother. Mass surveillance is everything and The Thought Police ensure no individual thinking is allowed. Winston Smith works at The Ministry of Truth, carefully rewriting history.

Moonshine by KAT BOSTICK

$19.99

There were three things Liv never wanted to be; starving, filthy, and alone. When an unexpected and inexplicable black out thrusts the world into chaos, that’s exactly how she finds herself. Running for her life, Liv falls at the feet of an unwilling savior. With a shack, a farm, and a lifetime of survival skills, Joshua is the perfect companion for the apocalypse. If only he wanted companionship. It’s hard to earn his trust and even harder to keep it. At a glance he seems to be nothing more than an off-kilter backwoods prepper with a grudge against humanity, but the longer she looks, the more Liv begins to see the man behind the churlish words and unkempt beard.

The Color Purple by ALICE WALKER

$19.99

A powerful cultural touchstone of modern American literature, The Color Purple depicts the lives of African American women in early twentieth-century rural Georgia. Separated as girls, sisters Celie and Nettie sustain their loyalty to and hope in each other across time, distance and silence. Through a series of letters spanning twenty years, first from Celie to God, then the sisters to each other despite the unknown, the novel draws readers into its rich and memorable portrayals of Celie, Nettie, Shug Avery and Sofia and their experience. The Color Purple broke the silence around domestic and sexual abuse, narrating the lives of women through their pain and struggle, companionship and growth, resilience and bravery. Deeply compassionate and beautifully imagined, Alice Walker's epic carries readers on a spirit-affirming journey towards redemption and love.

Top Authors

William Shakespeare

1564 to 1616

@shakespeare

William Shakespeare, often regarded as the greatest playwright in the English language, was born in Stratford-upon-Avon, England, in 1564. His works, encompassing tragedies, comedies, and histories, continue to captivate audiences worldwide. Shakespeare's profound understanding of human nature, coupled with his masterful use of language and intricate plots, has solidified his legacy as one of the most influential figures in literature. His timeless works, including "Hamlet," "Romeo and Juliet," and "Macbeth," explore themes of love, ambition, and the complexities of the human condition, ensuring his enduring relevance across generations.

Alice Walker

1994 to -

@aliceWalker

Alice Walker, a prominent American author and activist, was born in Eatonton, Georgia, in 1944. Renowned for her literary contributions and commitment to social justice, Walker's writing often explores themes of race, gender, and spirituality. Her Pulitzer Prize-winning novel, "The Color Purple," remains a seminal work in African American literature, celebrated for its powerful portrayal of resilience and sisterhood amidst oppression. Beyond her literary achievements, Walker's activism and advocacy continue to inspire generations, as she champions equality, environmentalism, and the empowerment of marginalized communities worldwide.

Frank Herbert

1920 to 1986

@frankherbert

Frank Herbert, a visionary science fiction author, was born in Tacoma, Washington, in 1920. Best known for his epic "Dune" series, Herbert's intricate world-building and exploration of complex themes have left an indelible mark on the genre. "Dune," a seminal work in science fiction, delves into politics, religion, and ecology on the desert planet of Arrakis, captivating readers with its depth and imagination. Herbert's legacy extends beyond his literary contributions; his insights into power dynamics and the human psyche continue to resonate, influencing both science fiction and contemporary discourse.
